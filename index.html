Methodology

  • Data Preprocessing: Meteorological data (2009–2023) for Romania, including air quality, temperature, and humidity.
  • Web Scraping: Extracted ~7,000 weather articles using Scrapy; processed with OpenAI API to map regions and phenomena.
  • Dataset Integration: Merged weather data and article insights using date-region keys.
  • Clustering: Applied K-Prototypes to group mixed numerical and categorical weather data into distinct clusters.

Limitations

  • Restricted to Romania; results may not generalize to other regions.
  • Dependent on data quality; errors or missing data could impact accuracy.
  • Focused on a specific period, potentially missing broader trends.
  • Methods like K-Prototypes and statistical tests have inherent weaknesses.

Datasets

  • Meteorological Data: Includes temperature, humidity, and PM10 levels (2009–2023).
  • News Articles: Weather phenomena narratives extracted from ~7,000 articles.
  • Clustered Data: Weather conditions grouped by K-Prototypes clustering.
  • Statistical Tests: Used to analyze trends and relationships, including Mann-Kendall and Spearman correlation.

Conclusion

This study integrates meteorological and narrative data to analyze air quality trends in Romania, showcasing complex weather interactions but limited by scope, time, and data reliability.
